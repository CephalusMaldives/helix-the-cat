MIDATA

What is midata?
A midata file downloaded from us contains a 12 month transaction history of your selected personal current account. If you’ve held your account for less than a year, it will contain data from the day you opened the account.
midata is presented in a format compatible for use on participating comparison websites, to help you better evaluate the range of current accounts available. The information in a midata file is limited to details about your incoming payments and your expenditure. It does not contain information like your name, address or date of birth.

Help & support

What can I find in pending transactions?

Pending transactions include recent debit card spend not yet taken from your account and cheques paid in that are still being processed.

Will all pending transactions come out of my account? Can I dispute payments?

No, not all pending transactions come out of your account. For example, you may see items you bought but returned, or checks made by companies to confirm your card is active. You can't dispute pending transactions. You can only dispute transactions once they appear on your statement and the funds have been debited.

Note: if you make a card purchase in a foreign currency the amount shown as a pending transaction may differ from the final amount debit from your account, depending on exchange rates.

How do pending transactions affect my balance and available funds?

Your account balance is a record of all transactions that have been applied to your account at a fixed point in time. It includes cheques paid in that are still being processed, but will not include any pending debit card transactions.

Your available funds show you the amount you have available to spend. This is your account balance plus any remaining overdraft limit, less pending debit card transactions. Cheques paid in that are still being processed are not included in your available funds.

How do I look for historic transactions?

You can either:

  • Click ‘load more transactions’ at the bottom of your statement page, when the ‘recent’ tab is highlighted
  • Click one of the previous three months in the calendar view at the top of your statement
  • Click the back (<) button for transactions in the three months before those in view
  • Or, to find older transactions, use the search tool.

What ways can I use the search tool to find transactions?

You can search by:

  • a date, or between two dates (maximum 90 day range at a time, so you can find what you need quickly)
  • a company or person’s name
  • the type of payment made: for example, just debit card transactions, or standing orders only

Can online statements be printed/saved as a PDF?

Yes, in the drop down list under Statement Options there is a ‘Print / download PDF’ function. Once you have selected a calendar month, it will display a full list of transactions in PDF format for you to print or download.

Can I export my statement to accounting software?

Yes. You can export all the transactions shown on your screen (loading more if you need to) or choose a range of transactions between two specific dates in either .CSV or .QIF format.

Can I order a copy of my statement in the post?

If you've gone paperless, you can order one copy of any historic statement (going back seven years) in the post, free of charge. If you still get paper statements, you can order an extra copy of one statement in the post free of charge – further orders, for extra copies of any statement, will be charged for. Remember, you can view, print and download transactions from your online statement at any time, at no cost.

How are my deposits protected?

Eligible deposits with us are protected by the Financial Services Compensation Scheme (FSCS). We will provide you with an information sheet and exclusions list every year. For further information about the compensation provided by the FSCS, refer to the FSCS website at www.FSCS.org.uk/

What is Balance Extra?

Balance Extra is a free service from Halifax that can help you manage your finances.

Please read our frequently asked questions and the eligibility criteria on our Balance Extra help page.

Where can I order a paper statement?

You can do that on the ‘Order paper statement’ page.

Where can I see all future payments pending on my account?

You can find that information on the future transactions statement.

Where can I see my full, cleared balance?

You can find that information on the projected cleared balance statement.

How can I order statements that are more than 12 months old?

Please call us on 0345 720 3040 (+44 1132 422 229 from outside the UK) to order back statements.

How do I report my card as lost or stolen?

Please fill in our online form or call us on 0800 015 1515 (+44 1132 428 196 from outside the UK) to report a lost or stolen card.

I need to let you know my travel plans. How do I do this?

You can tell us about your travel plans online, just fill in our simple form, which you can also find in the Menu of your Halifax Mobile Banking app. If you’d prefer you can call us on 0345 720 30 40 for your debit card, 0345 944 45 55 for your credit card or visit your nearest branch.

If you contact us online or via our automated telephone service, your travel plans will normally be displayed on Online Banking within 2 hours. If you tell us in branch or speak to one of our advisors on the telephone, the details won't show in Online Banking but they'll be logged immediately.

No matter how you tell us about your travel plans, rest assured we'll have a record of them and you'll be able to use your cards abroad with confidence.

I've been made redundant. What help is available to me?

If you’ve recently been made redundant or there’s a chance that you soon could be, our Redundancy help and guidance can help you to get an understanding of your finances and what support is available to you.

What is Cashback Extras?

Cashback Extras is a free of charge service for Online and Mobile Banking bank account customers. With it you'll get cashback offers that are based on the types of places you usually shop.

Just activate each offer in Online Banking for your next purchase, then shop with your Halifax debit card to get cashback paid into your bank account at the end of the next month.

What's more, you can also use your Halifax credit card when you shop, if you have one, and you'll get the cashback paid into your bank account if you're the primary account holder (the person whose name is on the bill). Spending by any additional cardholder will also count towards any cashback for the primary cardholder.

How do I close my savings account online?

Only savings accounts that are eligible can be closed online. To check if your account is eligible go to your Account Statement page and look for the ‘Close this account’ link. If you have an eligible savings account and a balance of £5 or less in your account you will be able to close your account online by clicking the ‘Close this account’ link, on the Account Statement page.

Why can’t I close my fixed rate or monthly saver account online?

Fixed rate and monthly saver accounts are structured to pay interest at the end of a fixed period. You may be charged for closing your account early. To protect you from accidentally incurring this charge we restrict online closure to eligible accounts. You can close your fixed rate or monthly saver account by visiting your nearest branch.

What if I have an applicable product but a balance of more than £5 and would like to close my savings account online?

If you have a savings account with a balance of more than £5 but wish to close your account online, you can transfer the money out of your savings account. Providing the transfer will leave you with a final balance of £5 or less, you will be presented with the opportunity to close your account online.

I have an account that is eligible and a balance of £5 or less, but I still don’t see the ‘Close this account’ link. How do I close my account?

There may be a restriction on your account that prevents changes from occurring to it, for security reasons such as periods of inactivity for 2 years or more. For further assistance please visit your nearest branch.

Can I close my joint account online?

Joint savings accounts can be closed online unless you’ve previously requested that both parties named on the account sign their names to authorise any changes. If this is the case, please visit your nearest branch to close your account.

How can I close my savings account if I have no other account to transfer the closing balance to?

If you only have your savings account with us and no other account to transfer your closing balance, please visit your nearest branch.

Will I get any outstanding or owed interest on my account if I close it online?

Yes, if you choose to close your savings account online we will calculate any remaining interest on the account and if applicable transfer it along with the remaining balance to an account chosen by you. A statement showing your closing balance will be shown on screen, once your account is closed. This screen can be printed or saved onto your computer.

What should I do if I’ve closed my account online but have not received the closing balance?

If you have closed your savings account online but not received your closing balance, please call us on this number 0345 602 0000 and we will try to find out why this has happened. If you prefer not to use our 0345 number, you can also call us on 0113 279 8302.

Where can I redeem my car loan offer voucher?

If you applied for a car loan between 2nd February and 31st March 2015 and subsequently drew down that loan, you will have received 12 car washes, a free MOT and 20% off a service. To find out where you can redeem these visit yourcarrewards.co.uk. You can also find out more by calling our helpline on 01565 831 128.
